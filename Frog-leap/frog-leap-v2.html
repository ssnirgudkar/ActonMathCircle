5 The Frog Leap
3 brown frogs sit on the left, 3 green frogs on the right, separated by one empty space. Brown frogs only move right →; green frogs only move left ←. A frog can slide one step into an empty space, or jump over exactly one frog of the opposite color into an empty space beyond. Can all frogs swap sides? Find the minimum number of moves.

Things to Discover

Conjecture freely
① Start small
Set frogs per side to 1. Play it. Now try 2. Now try 3. Each time, record how many moves it took. What do you notice? Can you predict the number of moves for 4 vs 4 before playing?
② Deadlock — what goes wrong?
Try to solve 3 vs 3 by moving frogs randomly. At some point you will get stuck — two frogs of the same color face each other across the empty space and neither can move. Why does this happen? What rule can you follow to never get stuck?
③ The rhythm
Look at the move log after a successful solution. Count how many brown moves and green moves appear in a row at each point. For n=3 the pattern of group sizes is 1, 2, 3, 2, 1. Does this pattern change for n=1 or n=2? What do you think it is for n=4?
